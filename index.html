Recent Publication

Effort can have positive, negative, and nonmonotonic impacts on outcome value in economic choice

Marcowski, P., Białaszek, W., & Winkielman, P.

Journal of Experimental Psychology: General (2025)

This paper investigates how effort influences outcome valuation in economic decision-making by contrasting prospective (anticipated) and retrospective (experienced) scenarios. The study demonstrates that effort can either diminish or enhance value depending on context and individual differences. To account for these complex effects, the authors introduce a novel dual‑system model that explains and predicts behavior in economic choices by capturing the dynamic interplay between effort costs and benefits.
